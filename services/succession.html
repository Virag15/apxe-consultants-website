Service 08

Succession & Exit Planning.

Book a discovery call

The hardest transitions in business aren’t the ones nobody planned, they’re the ones that were planned too late.

We work with founders and families to prepare the business, the next line of leadership, and the transaction (if there is one) before the clock forces the conversation. That means an honest view of what the business is actually worth today, what has to change to close the gap, and who on the inside is ready to carry it.

Succession isn’t a legal event. It’s a two-year operational project, with a transaction at the end if you want one.

Most of the work is unglamorous: tightening governance, de-risking customer concentration, writing down the institutional knowledge that currently lives in the founder’s head, and stress-testing the next layer of leadership in real decisions, not on paper. If a sale is the exit, the readiness work makes the difference between a discount and a premium. If it’s a family handover, the same work is what stops the business from stalling in year two.

We sit on your side of the table. We don’t take transaction fees, we don’t broker deals, and we don’t push an outcome. Our job is to make sure whichever exit you choose is a choice, not a forced move.

What this is

A transition, not a transaction.

A sale, a handover to the next generation, or stepping back into a chair role, they all look like events on the calendar. They’re not. Each one is a two-year project where the business has to be made less dependent on the founder, a second line has to be tested under real fire, and the numbers have to tell a story an outsider can actually underwrite.

We sit on your side of the table for that preparation. We assess where the business genuinely stands on founder-dependency, leadership depth, financial hygiene, and operational maturity. We build the plan to close the gaps, run the transition in stages, and, if a sale is the path, prepare the business to withstand diligence without surprises.

This is uncomfortable work. It involves honest conversations about family dynamics, about successor readiness, about what the founder actually wants next. We treat those conversations seriously and we don’t paper over the difficult parts.

What we solve

The problems we usually walk into.

If any of these sound familiar, you’re not alone, and they’re all fixable.

01

Founder is the business

Customers, key suppliers, and the best staff all report to the founder directly. If they step out for three months, the business wobbles.

02

No viable second line

Nobody inside the business is ready to take the top seat. The bench is thin, the talent is senior but narrow, and no one has been built for the role.

03

Family transition tension

The next generation is in the business but roles, authority, and timelines are fuzzy. Parents aren’t really letting go; children aren’t really stepping up.

04

Sale conversations starting

Brokers are calling, interest is real, but the books aren’t ready, the management team isn’t aligned, and there’s no agreed view on what a good deal looks like.

05

Valuation gap

What the owner thinks the business is worth and what the market will pay are 40% apart. Nobody has done the work to understand why or close it.

06

No plan for the founder

“After the exit” is undefined. Founders who don’t know what’s next tend to reverse course at the last minute, or disengage badly post-deal.

Our approach

How we actually build it.

Every engagement follows the same four-stage shape. The specifics change by sector, but the discipline doesn’t.

Step 01 01

Readiness assessment

Where does the business genuinely stand on founder-dependency, leadership, financial hygiene, and deal readiness? Honest baseline before any transition decisions get made.

Step 02 02

Path selection

Family succession, internal promotion, management buyout, strategic sale, financial investor, we work through the realistic paths and their implications with the owner.

Step 03 03

Prepare the business

Reduce founder-dependency, build the second line, clean the numbers, strengthen the operating model. The preparation work that makes the transition survivable.

Step 04 04

Execute the transition

Staged handover, diligence support if it’s a sale, board and governance changes, communication plan. We sit on your side through the event itself.

Who this is for

Fit matters more than fees.

We’d rather tell you this isn’t the right engagement than take a cheque and deliver nothing useful. Here’s how to tell.

This is right if you…

  • Are a founder or family owner thinking seriously about a transition in the next 2–5 years and want to do it properly.
  • Run a profitable, established business that has value worth preserving through the handover.
  • Are willing to have candid conversations about what the business actually depends on and what you actually want next.
  • Want an advisor on your side of the table, independent of brokers, investment bankers, and acquirers.

This is wrong if you…

  • Are looking for a broker to run a sale process. That’s an investment bank or M&A advisor’s job; we prepare, we don’t transact.
  • Want a quick valuation exercise. We can point you to people who do that, but our work is the preparation that makes the valuation higher.
  • Aren’t ready to step back, even partially. The plan only works if the founder genuinely commits to reducing their footprint.
  • Need immediate liquidity this year. This is a 6–12 month preparation engagement, not a distressed sale process.
FAQ

Common questions.

Most clients ask some version of these before we start. If yours isn’t here, bring it to the discovery call.

Do you sell the business for us?
No. We prepare the business, the owner, and the leadership team for a transition. When a sale process runs, it’s led by an investment bank, broker, or M&A advisor, and we’ll help you pick the right one. We stay on your side through diligence.
Can you work with family businesses?
A lot of our succession work is with family businesses. The technical work is similar; the relational work is harder. We’re willing to have the conversations about roles, fairness, and readiness that families often avoid, and we’ll bring in a family-business specialist counsellor where that’s the right tool.
Why does it take six to twelve months?
Because decoupling a business from a founder takes that long. Relationships have to transfer in real time. Second-line leaders need real reps. Financial hygiene takes two or three quarters to prove. Shorter engagements exist and usually underwhelm.
What if we decide not to sell?
Then you end up with a much better business run by much stronger leaders, and a founder who has room to step back when they want. Most of the work is valuable regardless of the endpoint, which is why we don’t push clients toward any particular path.
How do you work with our lawyer, accountant, banker?
We’re the business-side advisor coordinating alongside them. Lawyers handle legal structure, accountants handle tax efficiency, bankers run the deal. We keep the business preparation moving and make sure the owner isn’t the one juggling it all.
