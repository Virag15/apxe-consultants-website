How we think

Our operating philosophy.

01 / Senior-only

The person you meet is the person who does the work.

No leverage model, no associate teams, no bait-and-switch after the contract signs. If a senior can’t own the engagement end to end, we don’t take it. It’s the simplest quality control we know.

02 / Skin in the game

Fixed fees on scope we agreed together.

Hourly billing rewards slowness. We quote the work, not the clock. If we misjudge the scope, that’s on us, not a change order. It forces us to understand your business before we pitch a number.

03 / Bias to build

We stay until something is running.

Recommendations on a slide are not results. Every engagement ends with a working system, a trained owner on your side, and numbers that have moved. If we can’t stay through implementation, it isn’t our engagement to take.

Operating principles

How we actually work.

Four commitments that shape every engagement, from first call to final handover.

01

Fixed-fee, fixed-scope.

No hourly bills. No scope creep. The number we agree on the first call is the number on the invoice. If the work takes longer than we thought, that’s on us to solve.

02

One accountable senior.

The person who scopes the engagement is the person who delivers it. Your contact doesn’t change after signing. No handoff calls, no new faces halfway through.

03

We say no often.

Brief review, not brief acceptance. If the problem is misdiagnosed, the timing is wrong, or the outcome is unrealistic, we’ll say so in the first call. Fit first, fees second.

04

Outcomes over outputs.

We don’t count hours or slides. We count what moved. Pipeline, margin, conversion, cycle time. If the numbers don’t change, the engagement didn’t work, regardless of how polished the deck was.
