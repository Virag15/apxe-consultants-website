We use a small set of cookies to keep the site working and understand, in aggregate, what people read. We don’t use advertising cookies, we don’t track you across other websites, and we don’t sell data.

1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files that a website stores on your device. They let a site remember that you’ve visited before, what preferences you’ve set, and how you moved through the site during a session. Some cookies are essential for the site to work. Others help us understand usage.

2. What we use on this site

Our approach is minimal:

  • No advertising cookies.
  • No cross-site tracking.
  • Analytics only in aggregated, anonymised form.
  • A cookie-less privacy-friendly analytics provider where possible.

3. Types of cookies on this site

Strictly necessary

These are required for basic site functionality (for example, remembering if you’ve dismissed a notice). They don’t track you and can’t be turned off without breaking the site.

Analytics

We use privacy-friendly analytics to understand which pages are read and how people arrive at the site. These cookies (or cookie-less equivalents) do not identify you personally. Data is retained in aggregate for up to 26 months.

Preferences

If you’ve made a choice about cookies on this site, we’ll remember that choice for 12 months so we don’t ask again every visit.

4. Third-party cookies

A few third-party services may set their own cookies when you interact with them:

  • Calendly: when you use our discovery-call booking link, Calendly sets cookies to manage the booking session. See Calendly’s privacy policy.
  • Google Fonts: our typefaces are served via Google Fonts, which may log basic request data (IP, user agent). No cookies are set by this.

We don’t embed social media pixels, advertising networks, or cross-site trackers.

5. Controlling cookies

You have full control over cookies through your browser settings. In most browsers you can:

  • See what cookies are stored and delete them individually.
  • Block third-party cookies entirely.
  • Set preferences per-site.
  • Use private or incognito mode to prevent cookies being stored between sessions.

Note: blocking strictly-necessary cookies may break parts of the site (the booking flow, for example). Blocking analytics cookies won’t affect anything you see.
