How We Work

Four steps. Applied to every engagement. No shortcuts.

A lot of advisory firms are good at talking about what they do. Fewer are clear about how they actually do it.

Step 1

Discover

Every engagement starts with a proper discovery phase. Structured conversations with the people who actually know the business — often not just the founder, but the people running sales, finance, and operations day to day.

We are trying to understand three things: what the business is trying to achieve, what is currently getting in the way, and what has already been tried.

Step 2

Diagnose

Once we have a clear picture, we analyse it properly. We look at what the data says, how different parts connect, and where the real constraints sit.

A sales problem is sometimes a lead quality problem. A cash flow problem is sometimes a pricing problem. A succession challenge is often also a governance challenge.

Step 3

Design

We build the plan with you. Every recommendation is shaped by what we've learned — your team's capabilities, your market's dynamics, your resources, and your timelines.

Whatever the format, the goal is the same — something concrete your team can act on immediately.

Step 4

Deploy & Support

Implementation is where most advisory work falls apart. Plans change when they meet reality. Teams need to be trained. Systems need to be adjusted.

We stay engaged through this phase. Regular check-ins, hands-on support, and honest reassessment. We are not done until the work is running on its own.

What You Can Expect

Clear scope from day one

You will always know what we're working on, what we're responsible for, and what success looks like. We put this in writing at the start.

Regular, structured updates

We run formal progress reviews at each stage. You will never have to chase us to find out where things stand.

Straight talking

If something isn't working, or if we disagree with a direction, we say so. We are here to improve outcomes.

Knowledge stays with you

We build capability into your team throughout the engagement. When we leave, the understanding and the systems stay behind.
