What we won’t do

The engagements we turn down.

We’re better at saying no than most advisory firms. Here’s the work we won’t take, and why.

We don’t do 10-person steering committees.

If the decision needs ten people to sign off, the decision won’t happen. We work with one or two accountable owners, or we don’t start.

We don’t hand over a deck and leave.

Advisory that ends at the recommendation is a report, not a result. If we can’t stay through implementation, it’s not the right engagement for us.

We don’t take a brief we don’t believe in.

If the problem is misdiagnosed or the outcome is unrealistic, we’ll say so in the first call and point you to someone better placed. Fit first, fees second.

We don’t quote by the hour.

Hourly billing incentivises the wrong thing, taking longer. Every engagement is fixed-fee on scope agreed up front. If we underestimate, that’s on us.

We don’t subcontract the thinking.

The senior who scopes the work is the senior who does it. No bait-and-switch to a junior team after the contract is signed.
